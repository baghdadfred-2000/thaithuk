Australian man charged with murder of Thai teenager as Pattaya case moves to the courts

Published 28 June 2026 • Updated 7 July 2026 • Compiled from wire and local reporting

Thai police have arrested an Australian man over the death of a 17-year-old girl whose body was found inside a suitcase near a railway line close to Pattaya, in Chonburi province. He has been charged with murder, concealing a body, and taking a minor for an indecent purpose, and the case is now proceeding through the Thai courts.

The suspect, identified by police as Simon Carman, 46, was detained on the evening of Friday 26 June at Bangkok's Suvarnabhumi Airport as he prepared to board a flight to Perth, according to police and reporting by AFP and the Bangkok Post. He denies killing her, AFP reported.

Investigators said the girl was reported missing by friends on Friday afternoon. Officers traced her last known movements using CCTV footage, which they said showed her entering a condominium building with the suspect in the early hours of Thursday, and later showed the man leaving the building with a large black suitcase. Police alerted immigration officials, who intercepted him at the airport that evening.

The victim has been identified in Thai media reports as Tunchanok Donhomla, known by the nickname "Cake", a 17-year-old from Kalasin province in Thailand's Northeast. Her family and community in Kalasin have been mourning her loss, and relatives have said they want the legal process to take its course, according to Thai-language reporting.

Australia's Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade said it was providing consular assistance to an Australian detained in Thailand and could not comment further.

Police Colonel Anek Sarathongyu, chief of Pattaya City Police Station, told reporters that officers moved quickly from the missing-person report to the arrest within the same day, and that the investigation was continuing.

A note on this report: The accused has been charged, not convicted. Under Thai law, as elsewhere, he is presumed innocent unless and until a court finds otherwise. ThaiThuk will report the outcome of proceedings when they conclude. Our thoughts are with Tunchanok's family and community.
